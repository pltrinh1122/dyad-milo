## Slot derivations (why the bounds read as they do)

- **craft — a durable mental-fitness system.** Re-leveled from "a practice" up to "the
  durable system the dyad builds" (chiron's curriculum-as-artifact pattern). The subject is
  its client (recorded privately; generalization pinned). "CBT-grounded" was stripped from the
  essence: the grounding modality is not material to the craft — it is CBT only from the
  naming bias `dyad-cbt`, and is swappable.
- **craft_telos — the Operator sustains practice without the dyad.** Destination-first:
  locked the terminal state (self-restarting practice, the Operator no longer *needing* the
  dyad) before the path. Graduation is about *need*, not *use* — the Operator may keep using
  the dyad and tools; what ends is the dependency for reliability. Acceptance check: ~90%
  adherence with unaided self-recovery.
- **The scarce-resource re-level (load-bearing).** The hard part is not knowledge/skill
  (intellectual capability — table stakes) but **emotional capability** (behavior is driven
  by emotional responses, not just intellectual) and **mental-stamina** (day-in-day-out, and
  return after a lapse). "Mental" alone collapses intellectual and emotional; the distinction
  is kept in the slots even though the README may collapse it for accessibility.
- **craft_value — honesty over appearance.** Value-shaped, not rule-shaped. Facing an
  unflattering truth is an emotional act — it is where the emotional muscle is trained. Its
  enabling mechanism is compassion-toward-lapse (a mechanism, not a competing value).
- **craft_invariant — never lose compassion for human tendencies and relapse.** Operator's
  framing (human-first) chosen over the Agent's incentive-engineering draft. Compassion keeps
  honest reporting safe (protecting craft_value) and makes self-recovery possible.

## Failure-mode taxonomy (craft insight — PII-free)

The craft targets two failure modes, one upstream of the other:

- **Avoidance** *(upstream)* — the client avoids the mentally-taxing activity *before
  starting*, because of the discomfort and friction it brings. The discomfort avoided is
  exactly the training load; avoidance forfeits the rep.
- **Lapse** *(downstream)* — after beginning, the client falls out of practice, then conceals
  the lapse because it can't be borne; a concealed lapse can't be trained.

Both are the same emotional-capability + mental-stamina shortfall. The system's two jobs follow:
lower the activation cost of *approaching* (against avoidance), and keep lapse *safe to report*
(against concealment).

Client-specific grounding, evidence, and practice data are **not** retained here — they live in
the private client record (see `DYAD.md § Externality`). Proof is self-asserted at n=1; the
personal details are not required in the dyad's public ledger.

## Claim ladder: the problem/solution dis-conflation (2026-07-15)

Promoting the indictment to **Claim 0** (Operator disposition) dis-conflates the belief — which
conflates problem+solution in one headline line — into two atomic claims held intentionally apart:

- **Claim 0 — the problem (default-problem).** Left to the default (willpower + technique),
  practice fails at adherence. Falsifier-space: *the status quo suffices; no deliberate system is
  needed.*
- **Claim 1 — the solution-locus.** The mind is the binding constraint, so that is what the craft
  builds. Falsifier-space: *the binding constraint is something other than mental-fitness.*

**Why they stay separate — atomicity = rep-honesty (load-bearing; future-proofs against a "why not
merge these?" edit).** The two occupy distinct falsifier-spaces (each carries its own knife), so
they *cannot* merge without producing a claim that fails its own falsifier — the separation is
mechanical, not stylistic. It doubles as an **intentional-frictional-rep** (Operator's frame):
keeping problem and solution apart makes the reader re-derive the link, the deliberate friction
milo's craft prescribes against wetware staleness. The guard that keeps the rep honest is the same
atomicity test — friction is a *rep* only when it exercises a real muscle (re-deriving a
load-bearing link); splitting a genuinely-single claim would be *empty* friction, a forfeited rep
("the discomfort avoided is the training load" said back to the form). Atomicity and rep-honesty
are one test viewed twice; the ladder's form instances the craft it argues.

**Wu-wei — leave the split unannotated in the README lens.** The rep works by making the reader
re-derive the problem→solution link; labeling it ("note the split") spoon-feeds the derivation and
forfeits the friction. Two claims, ordered 0→1, no meta-commentary. Explicitness is fine in the
anchor (substrate, not lens).

**Anchor architecture — the problem precedes the craft (Operator disposition; home = option "b").**
Claim 0 is logically prior to `§ Craft`: the craft *exists to answer* the problem. So the anchor
gains a **`§ Problem`** section (working name) positioned **before `§ Craft`**, and Claim 0 homes
there — not inside § Craft. Lock-step, landing anchor-first (source before derived view): the
anchor edit is its own reviewed PR; the README's Claim 0 home, `canonical_home`, and
`belief.foundation` gain `§ Problem` in the review branch.
